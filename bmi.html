What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It helps assess whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or greater

Why is BMI Important?

Maintaining a healthy BMI can reduce the risk of health problems such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure. A balanced diet and regular exercise are key to staying within a healthy BMI range.

How to Improve Your BMI?

Here are some tips to maintain a healthy BMI:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and proteins.
  • Exercise regularly, including cardio and strength training.
  • Stay hydrated and get enough sleep.
  • Avoid excessive junk food and sugary drinks.
